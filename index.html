The idea in one paragraph

Dollar-cost averaging — buying a fixed amount every week — is already one of the best strategies for long-term accumulators, because it removes emotion and timing. DCA Compass keeps that habit and adds a volume knob: when Bitcoin trades at levels that history marks as unusually cheap (at its 200-week average, deep below its 200-day average), the rules say buy 2–3× your base amount; when it's historically stretched, they say slow down or pause. It never predicts. It only measures where today's price sits relative to Bitcoin's own long-term averages — and shows you exactly why, with every number visible.

What you see when you open it

One number for this week

A single multiplier from 0× to 3×, decided by six transparent rules. Every rule shows its live values and a ✓ or ✗ — you can check the logic yourself in ten seconds.

Four classic indicators

The 200-week moving average, the Mayer Multiple, the Bull Market Support Band, and the Pi Cycle ratio — each explained in plain language, with a beginner's guide built in.

Rare "mania insurance"

Two sell rules that only trigger at historic extremes (reached in 2013, 2017 and 2021 — never since). If markets stay calm, they never fire and cost nothing.

Questions people ask

Is this financial advice?

No. DCA Compass is an educational tool that shows what a fixed, published set of rules would do. It knows nothing about you, your finances, or your goals, and it never tells you what you should do. Whether and how much to invest is entirely your decision — ideally one you'd discuss with a qualified advisor.

Why does it almost never sell?

Because we tested selling, honestly, and it failed: every profit-taking threshold low enough to trigger in modern, calmer cycles also fired during ordinary rallies, selling coins that should have been kept. Only extreme "mania" levels historically marked true tops, so those are the only sell triggers left — and they may never fire again. The full negative results are on the methodology page.

Where does the data come from?

Your own browser fetches daily prices directly from Coinbase (with a Binance fallback) every time you open the page. There's no server in between, no account, and no tracking — the tool is a single self-contained page.

Why is it free?

The rules only have value if they're transparent, and transparency means anyone can rebuild them anyway. If enough people find it useful, optional extras like weekly signal alerts may come later — the dashboard itself stays free.

Does this work for other assets?

The architecture does, but the thresholds don't transfer — they must be recalibrated to each asset's own history, and calmer assets have far less mispricing to harvest. We've calibrated a world-equity-ETF version; its honest backtested edge is small (~+1.3% per euro vs plain DCA), exactly as theory predicts.
