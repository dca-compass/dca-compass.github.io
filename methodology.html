Methodology

Everything below is published so you can verify, criticize, or rebuild the strategy yourself. If you find an error, we want to know about it.

Data

Historical research used CoinMetrics community data (daily BTC-USD reference rates from July 2010) spliced with Coinbase Exchange daily candles for recent periods. The live dashboard fetches ~6.3 years of daily candles directly from Coinbase (Binance as fallback) in your browser on every page load; nothing is proxied or stored. All series are calendar-daily. Indicator windows are calendar days (200-week SMA = 1,400 daily closes).

Indicators

  • 200-week simple moving average — mean of the last 1,400 daily closes. Historically the deepest bear-market lows (2015, 2018, 2020, 2022, 2026) occurred at or near this line.
  • Mayer Multiple — price ÷ 200-day SMA.
  • Bull Market Support Band — the band between the 20-week SMA and 21-week EMA of weekly (Sunday) closes.
  • Pi Cycle ratio — 111-day SMA ÷ (2 × 350-day SMA). The classic "Pi Cycle Top" is this ratio crossing 1.0; it marked the 2013, 2017 and 2021 tops within days and has not crossed since.

The rules (complete specification)

Decisions are weekly, on Mondays, always using the previous day's indicator values (no lookahead). The first matching rule sets the week's DCA multiplier:

PriorityConditionAction
1price ≤ 1.05 × 200W SMAbuy 3× base
2price ≤ 1.15 × 200W SMAbuy 2× base
3Mayer < 0.8buy 2× base
4Mayer > 1.65buy 0× (pause)
5Mayer > 1.40buy 0.5× base
6price < BMSB lower band and Mayer < 1.2buy 1.5× base
7otherwisebuy 1× base

Changelog: rule 2 (graduated 2× within 15% of the 200W SMA) added July 2026, before public launch, to smooth the exit from the crisis zone; it improved backtested per-dollar efficiency from +44.9% to +46.8% vs plain DCA. Rules are never changed silently.

Mania insurance (sell rules), each firing at most once per cycle, re-armed when Mayer < 0.8: Mayer crossing above 2.40 → sell 10% of holdings; Pi Cycle ratio crossing above 1.00 → sell 10%. Proceeds are held as a cash reserve and redeployed at 25% of the reserve per week while price ≤ 1.05 × 200W SMA, or 10% per week while Mayer < 0.8.

Backtest protocol

Simulation period June 2014 – July 2026 (constrained by the 200W SMA needing 1,400 days of prior data). $100/week base amount. The benchmark is plain DCA: $100 every Monday, never selling. No fees, spreads, or taxes are modeled — real-world results would be lower for both strategies, and slightly more so for the strategy (it trades marginally more). All code paths use lagged indicators; indicator math was independently verified in two implementations (Python and JavaScript) to the cent.

What we tested that did NOT work

We consider this section the most important one on the page.

  • Frequent profit-taking destroys value. A four-tranche sell strategy (25% at Mayer 1.4 / 1.65, Pi > 0.70, and a trend-break) backtested at 28.9× vs 46.5× for never selling — worse than plain DCA per dollar. Sell levels low enough to fire in modern cycles also fire mid-rally.
  • Indicator dampening is real and accelerating. Cycle-peak Mayer values: 13.1 (2011) → 8.3 (2013) → 3.8 (2017) → 2.8 (2021) → 1.85 (2025). The Pi Cycle has not crossed since 2021 and may never cross again. This is why the sell rules are "insurance," not strategy: expected to stay silent.
  • All-in reserve deployment failed. A "deploy everything on trend recovery" rule dumped the entire reserve near a local top in Aug 2021. Only gradual, tranche-based redeployment survived testing.

Limitations — read these as carefully as the results

  • Bitcoin has completed only ~4 market cycles. Every threshold here is fitted to a tiny sample.
  • The strategy's edge depends on Bitcoin continuing to cycle around a rising long-term trend. If that regime ends — in either direction — no moving average will announce it in advance.
  • Indicator compression means buy zones will also drift: Mayer cycle-lows are rising (0.23 → 0.62). The 2× zone may need recalibration around 2030; we review annually.
  • Backtests exclude fees, taxes, slippage, and — most importantly — the psychology of actually buying 3× during a −75% drawdown. The simulated investor never flinches. Real ones do.
  • Historical simulation is not evidence of future performance. It is evidence of past performance only.
